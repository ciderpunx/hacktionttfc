Tech Tactics for Social Change

Oxford Hackspace: Saturday 21 and Sunday 22 Oxtober 2017

Technology is all around us. It pervades our work, our play, and our personal lives. It is the vehicle for our voices, our desires, our hopes, our ideas, our outrage. Our spiritual, social and metaphysical selves exist inside it.

The digital age is both emancipating and enslaving; both a liberation and a burden. It has given us freedom to communicate our ideas and also made it harder to communicate them freely without being monitored. It is our best hope and our worst nightmare.

Through two days of talks at the Oxford Hackspace in October this year we will:

  • Exchange ideas about what living in the digital age means for those on the margins and those who campaign and work to help them
  • Look at what is needed and ways to solve those needs through tools and practices
  • Explore and how we can work together to make our ideas and dreams a reality

We will focus on five key areas for discussion:

  • Gender
  • Refugee solidarity
  • Housing & homelessness
  • Sustainability
  • Avoiding burnout

If you would like to present please contact us.

Please note that HacktionLab is a volunteer-run not-for-profit organisation with no funding other than direct contributions from members and those who attend our events. We are therefore unable to pay any expenses for attending or participating in the event and do ask for a small contribution in the form of a donation to cover costs. This also means that the event is also free to those with little or no income.

  • Get in touch!

If you or your organisation works in any of the above areas, or if you work in digital technologies and think you can help such organising, do get in touch. We are currently putting the programme for the two days together and asking for sessions of up to 45 minutes in the form of a presentation/talk, facilitated discussion, demonstration, show and tell, group activity, or simple workshop.
